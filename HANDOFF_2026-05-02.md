## User's overall complaint to address

> "A lot of the shaders just feel kind of cheap and rendered cheaply. I'm not really getting this high premium render quality I would expect from Unreal or TouchDesigner. Some of these shaders are meant to be rendered in browsers so they were cautious about features. I want to feel like a premium product."

Translation: many shaders are SDFs with flat shading, no AO, no specular, no global illumination approximations. The high-impact upgrades are:
- Real two-light + ambient occlusion + cast shadow on raymarched scenes
- Multi-tap blur for soft shadows / glow
- Specular highlights + fresnel on every 3D surface
- Multi-octave fbm for organic noise instead of single-tap value noise
- Dithering / film grain post step
- Tone mapping (ACES, not just clamp)
- Higher default counts where loops allow (more particles, more cubes, more facets)

The autonomous loop's fix templates should be expanded with a "premium" axis: every patch should ALSO check whether the shader has these features and add them if missing.
